## Page Body

### Water Needs Somewhere to Go

When it rains hard in north Alabama, some yards just don't drain the way they should. Low spots fill with water. Downspouts dump runoff against the foundation. Water pools near driveways and walkways for days after a storm.

This isn't just a nuisance — it's a problem that compounds over time. Standing water damages grass and plant roots, accelerates foundation issues, creates breeding grounds for mosquitoes, and can eventually cause structural damage to your home.

Sprinkler Medic diagnoses drainage problems and installs solutions that move water efficiently off your property — for good.

### Drainage Issues We Solve

- **Yard ponding and standing water** — low spots that collect water after rain
- **Foundation drainage problems** — water pooling against or near your home's foundation
- **Soggy or waterlogged lawn areas** — areas that never seem to dry out
- **Driveway and walkway runoff** — water sheeting across hardscapes
- **Downspout drainage** — improper discharge that saturates soil near the house
- **Slope and grading issues** — terrain that directs water toward structures

### Solutions We Install

**French Drains**
A perforated pipe wrapped in filter fabric, buried in a gravel-filled trench. Water seeps into the trench and is carried underground to a safe discharge point. Effective for large-area drainage and subsurface water management.

**Catch Basins**
Surface-level drains that capture water from low spots and direct it through underground pipe to a discharge location. Ideal for lawn depressions, driveway edges, and patio areas.

**Channel Drains**
Linear surface drains installed across driveways, patios, or paths to capture sheet flow before it accumulates or flows toward structures.

**Downspout Extensions and Buried Discharge Lines**
Route downspout water away from your foundation through buried pipe, discharging at a safe distance or into a drainage system.

**Regrading**
Sometimes the most effective fix is reshaping the terrain. We assess and regrade problem areas to direct water flow away from structures and toward proper drainage.

### Our Process

We start with a site assessment — walking the property during or after rain conditions when possible, or reviewing how water moves based on your description and the terrain. We identify the primary problem areas and design a solution sized to your yard's actual drainage load.

You'll get a clear explanation of what we propose and why before any work starts.
